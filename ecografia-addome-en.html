Abdominal ultrasound in Pozzallo

A thorough exam to check the abdominal organs: liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, kidneys, bladder. Quick, painless and without radiation. By appointment in Pozzallo (RG), Italy, with a report and explanation at the end.

What it is and what it is for

Abdominal ultrasound lets us look at the internal organs and assess their shape, size and structure. It is useful for checking the liver and biliary tract (including gallstones), the pancreas, the spleen, the kidneys and the bladder, and to investigate abdominal pain, abnormal blood tests or routine check-ups.

Upper, lower or complete abdomen

The upper abdomen examines the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen and kidneys; the lower abdomen the bladder and pelvic organs; the complete abdomen includes both. Your doctor's referral usually states which one is needed; if you have any doubts, call me and we'll check together.

How to prepare

Preparation depends on the exam:

  • Upper or complete abdomen: fast for 6-8 hours (so the gallbladder and organs are seen more clearly).
  • Lower abdomen / pelvis: full bladder — drink about a litre of water one hour before and do not urinate until the exam.

If you're unsure about your preparation, call me before the appointment.

How it works

You lie down and I place the probe on your abdomen with a little gel. No needles, no radiation, no discomfort. In a few minutes the exam is complete and I give you the report, explaining in plain words what was found.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to fast?

For the upper or complete abdomen, yes — fast for 6-8 hours. For the lower abdomen a full bladder is needed instead.

What is the difference between upper, lower and complete?

The upper examines the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen and kidneys; the lower the bladder and pelvis; the complete both.

Does the exam hurt or use radiation?

No: it is painless and uses ultrasound waves, not radiation. It is safe and repeatable.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

For a private exam it is not required. If you have one, bring it along with any previous scans.
