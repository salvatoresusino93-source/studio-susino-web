Lower-limb Doppler ultrasound in Pozzallo

The exam that studies the circulation of the veins and arteries of the legs: useful for swollen or heavy legs, varicose veins and vascular prevention. Painless, with no needles or radiation. By appointment in Pozzallo (RG), Italy, with a report at the end.

What it is and what it is for

Doppler ultrasound combines ultrasound with the study of blood flow: it lets us see how blood moves through the veins and arteries of the legs. It is useful for understanding the cause of swelling, heaviness, varicose veins, for suspected thrombosis and for vascular prevention.

Venous or arterial

The venous Doppler studies the veins (varicose veins, swelling, suspected thrombosis); the arterial Doppler the arterial circulation (for example, leg pain while walking). Your doctor's referral indicates which is needed: if you have any doubts, call me and we'll check.

When to have it

Legs that are swollen or heavy at the end of the day, visible veins appearing, cramps or calf pain, a family history of venous problems: these are all situations where this exam helps to clarify things.

How it works and preparation

No preparation is needed. I make you comfortable and study the circulation by placing the probe on your leg with a little gel. No needles, no radiation, no discomfort. At the end I give you the report and explain what was found.

Frequently asked questions

What exactly is it for?

To assess the circulation of the veins and arteries of the legs: swelling, varicose veins, suspected thrombosis, prevention.

Does it hurt? Is preparation needed?

No to both: it is painless, with no needles or radiation, and it requires no preparation.

Does it study the veins or the arteries?

It depends on the exam requested: there is a venous Doppler and an arterial one. The referral indicates which; if in doubt, call.

Do I need a doctor's referral?

For a private exam it is not required. If you have one, bring it along with any previous scans.
