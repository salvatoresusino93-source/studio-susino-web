Musculoskeletal ultrasound in Pozzallo

The exam that studies muscles, tendons and joints, even in movement: useful for pain that won't go away and for sports injuries. Painless and without radiation. By appointment in Pozzallo (RG), Italy, with a report at the end.

What it is and what it is for

Musculoskeletal ultrasound lets us look at muscles, tendons, ligaments and joints, with the advantage of being able to study them in movement. It is very useful for pain that won't go away, tendinitis, bursitis, effusions and sports injuries.

Which parts can be examined

Shoulder, elbow, wrist and hand, hip, knee, ankle and foot, as well as muscles and tendons in general. Your doctor's referral states the area to study; if you have any doubts, call me and we'll check together.

When to have it

Shoulder or knee pain lasting for weeks, a suspected strain or tendinitis, a sports trauma: these are all situations where this exam helps understand what is happening, quickly and without radiation.

How it works and preparation

No preparation is needed. I examine the area by placing the probe with a little gel, sometimes asking you to move the joint to assess it better. No needles, no radiation. At the end I give you the report and explain what was found.

Frequently asked questions

What can be seen with this exam?

Muscles, tendons, ligaments and joints, even in movement: useful for pain, tendinitis, bursitis and sports injuries.

Does it hurt? Does it use radiation?

No: it is painless and uses ultrasound waves, not radiation.

Which parts of the body can be studied?

Shoulder, elbow, wrist/hand, hip, knee, ankle/foot and muscles/tendons in general.

Is preparation or a referral needed?

No preparation. A referral is not required for a private exam; if you have one, bring it along with any previous scans.
